June's Answers

To recap, June's language puzzle was:

Starting with a two letter word, add another letter to make a valid word. Keep adding letters, one at a time, each time the word must be valid. The letter may be added anywhere. Each word must be in a standard dictionary. For example, To, Ton, Tone, Stone, Stoner, Stonier etc. What is the longest sequence that can be made?

The winning entries were:
in, sin, sing, singe, singer, stinger, stingers, stringers
on, one, hone, hones, honest, honesty, honestly
an, pan, pane, paner, panner, spanner, spanners

To recap, June's logic puzzle was:

What number comes next in this sequence: 101 65 131 116 131 1021 1021 ==?==

Answer:

121; Starting with January, convert the first two letters to their position in the alphabet, J A = 10 1. February, FE = 65, etc. Therefore AUGUST, AU = 121.
